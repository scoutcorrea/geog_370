Homework 3

In this project I learned how to display images in different projections.

The first thing I did to make the projections was install the Indicatrix mapper plug-in. Next, I created a vector layer from the world projections folder in Sakai. Specifically, I used ne_10m_admin_0_countries in Shapefile format. Using the default settings, the Indicatrix mapper added longitude and latitude lines and generated circles. After clicking the button in the bottom right corner of the QGIS screen, I changed the projection type after searching for the projection name in the filter bar. Changing the projection altered the lines and circles. The differences in circle shape and size is a reflection of the distortions each type of projection had to make. After each projection, I created a new print layout, added text, and exported the image as a PNG.

WGS 84 / Pseudo-Mercator Projection

This projection maintained shape and distance, but area and direction were distorted. Area increases greatly closer to the top and bottom of the map.

WGS 84 Projection

This projection did not preserve shape, area, or direction. However, distance was the same. Shape was preserved until the top and bottom circles become more distorted.

Sphere Winkel I Projection

This projection preserved shape and direction. Area and distance are slightly distorted towards the edges of the map.

World Polyconic Projection

World Polyconic did not really preserve shape, area, direction, or distance well. Distortion gets worse closer to the edges of the projection.

World Equidistant Conic Projection

This projection preserves shape, area, distance, and direction well closest to the North Pole. As it gets further away, all of those properties become more distorted.

World Robinson Projection

This projection preserves shape and area closer to the Equator. As it goes further, area increases and the shape becomes more distorted. Direction are distance are preserved.

World Cylindrical Equal Area Projection

Like the one before, this projection preserves shape, area, and direction pretty well around the Equator. Shape and area are very distorted closer to the Poles. Distance is closer to the Equator.

North Pole Azimuthal Equidistant Projection

This projection is very similar to the World Equidistant Conic. Shape and area are better preserved by the North Pole, but by the South Pole everything becomes more distorted. Direction, distance, area, and shape are increasingly distorted.

The World from Space Projection

The other projections attempt to visualize a sphere in a flat way to display all of the continents. One drawback of this projection, despite having no distortion, is that certain continents are not displayed.
